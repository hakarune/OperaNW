About Opera Quest Northwest

Opera Quest Northwest is a newly incorporated nonprofit, and the designated successor to the Vancouver Children’s Opera. We are building and expanding on that organization’s 12-year heritage of presenting live opera to young audiences.

Our mission is “to educate, enrich and instill a love of music in the children of our region by introducing them to opera through a lively production that blends classic opera excerpts with a humorous, interactive script.”

With the help of grants and private donations, we offer our production “Opera the Great” to elementary schools at no cost to the school. “Opera the Great” is a live program featuring five young professional singers and a pianist.

Over the past three years, we have presented it throughout Vancouver’s Evergreen School District to the delight of hundreds of 3rd through 5th graders. At the end of the show, the children are able to ask any opera-related questions they may have.

An Orchards Elementary teacher was impressed with the production. “It really awakened my students’ appreciation for classical music performance. Besides this, it’s funny, witty and engaging. My students talked about it for weeks afterward, and even acted out their favorite parts,” the teacher said.

Educating young people is the whole purpose of “Opera the Great.” We believe passionately in the power of music in education; and in fact, studies confirm that participating in music activities helps children excel academically. Supplementary programs like ours are especially vital in a time when many schools are reducing or even eliminating funding for music and the arts.

Don Nelson, fine arts manager for Evergreen Public Schools, agrees on the impact “Opera the Great” has on these students, “After three years of having ‘Opera the Great’ in our schools, I can say that every principal consistently tells me this is one of the most engaging and delightful assemblies they have hosted,” Nelson said.

Music enriches the soul of a community, yet many families do not have the resources to expose their children to live classical music. That’s where we come in; by bringing it into our schools, we are planting the seeds for a lifelong appreciation of—and support for—the performing arts that will benefit current and future generations.

Our performers are all educators by nature, training and profession. Our program has specific educational goals: for example, students learn opera vocabulary, history, characters, voice parts and famous melodies. Since children learn best through participation, our script lets them try out operatic gestures and singing styles and become part of the chorus. Some even have the chance to come onstage as opera characters.

Member Bios


Amy Cole – Soprano

Amy plays the Ingenue in “Opera the Great.” A private vocal teacher, she has sung roles in several operas including Emmy in Albert Herring and Armchair in L’enfant et les Sortileges with Portland Opera, and Gretel in Hansel and Gretel with Vancouver Children’s Opera. She has been a featured soloist with the Oregon and Vancouver symphonies, performed at New York’s Carnegie Hall, toured Europe with the Portland State University Chamber Choir and was soprano soloist on the CD On American Shores.

ChristyAnne Ford – Mezzo Soprano

ChristyAnne co-authored “Opera the Great” and performs the two mezzo soprano roles. Her work throughout the Northwest spans gospel, musical theater, oratorio, opera and recital repertoire; she has performed with Lakewood Theater, Opera Theater of Oregon, Portland Chamber Orchestra, and the Olympia, Walla Walla and Columbia symphonies. She has worked with such renowned artists as Tito Capobianco and Marilyn Horne, and won first place at the district Metropolitan Opera auditions in 2004.

Kathryn Hobbie

A longtime board member of the Vancouver Symphony, Kathryn is the pianist who serves as the “orchestra” for “Opera the Great.” She has performed with a variety of instrumentalists and vocalists including the Vancouver Symphony, VSO Chamber Series, Vancouver Children’s Opera, and Olympia Chorale and Light Opera; she toured Europe and performed at Boston’s Symphony Hall with the Chamber Singers of Mount Holyoke College, where she earned her music degree.

Bruce Kyte – Baritone

Bruce sings the role of the Buffo in “Opera the Great.” Since 2005 he has performed as a singer and actor in a wide variety of productions in Portland/Vancouver. His favorites include Rent (the role of Angel) at Clark College, selections from Don Giovanni with the Clark College Opera Workshop, and Aida with Portland Opera. He has studied with April Duvic at Clark College and Thomas Harper at the University of Washington.

Patrick Scofield – Bass Baritone

Patrick plays the, sometimes mustachioed, Villain in “Opera the Great.” He is music director of the Unitarian Universalist Church of Vancouver and the Elsie Stuhr Senior Center in Beaverton, co-leads the summer program at Portland’s First Unitarian Church and serves as director of conferences for the UU Musicians Network. Patrick has sung in the Portland Opera Chorus since 1997 and performs with many other groups including The Dickens Carolers. His degree is in voice and music education from Pacific University.

David Warner – Tenor

David has performed with the Portland Opera Chorus since 2010 and sang roles in Portland Opera’s Candide and Salome. His theatrical debut was as Judas in the Clark College production of Jesus Christ Superstar, which he performed while simultaneously singing in the chorus of Portland Opera’s Aida. He is studying voice with April Duvic at Clark College.
